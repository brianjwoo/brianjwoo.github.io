About Me

I currently work at Galvanize as a Data Science in Residence. Previously, I have worked on a number of research projects to gain insight into the progression of diseases such as atherosclerosis and cancer. For a number of years, I studied effect of small RNAs in regulating gene expression and their disregulation could affect downstream processes. During this time, I also worked in a small group that was looking for the effects of AMPK activation in a similar context, which controls energy utilization in the cell. I later worked in a bioinformatics lab developing a sequencing based assay for detection of DNA adducts at a single nucleotide resolution to gain insight into the effects of DNA-damaging agents on cancer cells. During this time, I also contributed to a number of cancer related projects by processing and anayzing array data to draw insights for future projects.
While working as a research assistant in a bioinformatics lab, I utilized my free time to familiarize myself with the fundamental skills of a data scientist. Although much of my work on the bioinformatics side involved the use of R and bash. I have completed over 20 MOOCs offered by Coursera and Edx to facilitate the transition from benchtop scientist into a computer/data scientist.
